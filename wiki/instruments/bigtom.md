# BigTOM

> [!info] In plain terms
> A bigger, more naturalistic theory-of-mind test set than the classic toy "Sally–Anne"
> stories. Instead of only "an object moved while you weren't looking," its stories are built
> from a recipe — a character *wants* something, *perceives* something, forms a *belief*, then
> an *unexpected event* changes the world — and the test asks what the character now believes
> or will do. A quirk to watch: the stories were written by GPT-4, so evaluating GPT-4 on it
> is partly self-grading.

## What It Measures

Theory-of-mind / false-belief reasoning ([[theories/theory-of-mind]]) over a **larger task
space** than object-location change, in "more natural language and social settings" than ToMI.
BigTOM (Gandhi et al. 2023) is a Sally–Anne false-belief-style benchmark generated from a
**causal template**: it defines an agent's **desire, percept, and initial belief**, introduces
a **causal event** that changes the environment, and generates the resulting agent **belief or
action**. Templates are authored by humans; the surface text is **generated using GPT-4**.

## Administration

- As used by [[sources/wilf-et-al-2024]]: the **Forward Action** and **Forward Belief**
  question types are used (not **Backward Belief**), to mirror the structure of ToMI. BigTOM is
  **balanced** at **200 questions per type**, so no sampling is needed. It is a **binary
  multiple-choice** task (random accuracy 50%). The target character name is parsed
  deterministically as the **first word of each story**.
- "False Belief" in results = averaging Forward Action + Belief; "All" = across all types
  considered. Released under the MIT license.

## Validity Notes

- **GPT-4-authored confound:** because BigTOM's stories were generated by GPT-4, evaluating
  GPT-4 on it risks measuring self-consistency rather than theory of mind; GPT-4 already
  **saturates human performance** on BigTOM 0-shot (Gandhi et al. 2023), which is why
  perspective-taking interventions show little/no effect on GPT-4 here
  ([[paradigms/simtom-perspective-taking-prompting]]).
- **Synthetic generation:** items are model-generated from templates — naturalistic surface,
  but still a fixed causal schema; contamination/saturation concerns as for any canonical ToM
  format ([[paradigms/false-belief-task]]).
- Forward (Action/Belief) vs. Backward Belief question types differ in difficulty; the study
  restricts to Forward types for comparability with ToMI.
