# Gender/Sex Diversity Beliefs Scale (GSDB)

> [!info] In plain terms
> A questionnaire measuring what someone believes about the nature of gender and sex — for
> example, whether they affirm that diverse gender identities exist and are valid, or instead
> see people of the same sex as basically alike. Used here to surface prejudice toward gender
> and sex minorities encoded in models.

## What It Measures

Beliefs about the ontology of gender and sex (Schudson & van Anders 2022), structured into
**five factors**:

- **Affirmation** — recognizing the existence of gender/sex diversity (items denying it load
  negatively).
- **Gender normativity** — importance of femininity for women / masculinity for men, and the
  "inauthenticity" of non-normative gender expression.
- **Uniformity** — that people of the same gender or sex are similar to each other.
- **Surgery** — that genital surgery is a necessary precondition for "truly" transitioning.
- **Upbringing** — the role of upbringing/early experience in determining gender or sex.

All factors except upbringing carry a positive (affirmation) or negative (gender normativity,
uniformity, surgery) valence toward gender/sex minorities.

## Administration

Self-report scale over the five factors. Administered to LLMs via
[[paradigms/psychometric-inventory-administration]].

## Validity Notes

- The affirmation/uniformity factors give a direct read on prejudice vs. affirmation toward
  gender and sex minorities.
- In [[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]] all models shared an emphasis on **uniformity** and a
  **lack of affirmation** (e.g. disagreeing with "Nonbinary gender identities are valid"),
  pointing to a shared non-affirming bias across models.
