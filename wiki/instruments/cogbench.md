# CogBench

> [!info] In plain terms
> A ready-made battery of psychology-lab tasks packaged as an AI benchmark — "a large
> language model walks into a psychology lab." Its special trick is that test items are
> generated by procedure rather than copied from a fixed list, so a model can't have
> memorized the answers from its training data.

> [!note] Survey-depth page
> Known here only via [[hagendorff-et-al-2024]]; enrich from
> the primary source (Coda-Forno et al. 2024) when ingested.

## What It Measures

Behavioral/cognitive profiles of LLMs using tasks from the experimental-psychology
tradition (the review cites it among heuristics-and-biases-style studies of LLM
decision-making).

## Administration

Administered as prompts; items can be "procedurally generated (instead of consisting of
a static dataset)."

## Used In (paradigms)

- [[paradigms/heuristics-and-biases-battery]]

## Validity Notes

- Procedural generation makes it "inherently less susceptible to data contamination
  issues" — the review's recommended antidote to the contamination problem that plagues
  canonical psychology items.
