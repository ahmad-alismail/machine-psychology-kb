# Revised Portrait Values Questionnaire (PVQ-RR)

> [!info] In plain terms
> A values survey with a twist: instead of asking "how important is creativity to you?", it
> describes a *person* ("Thinking up new ideas and being creative is important to her") and
> asks how much you resemble them. Because it ships in a "he" and a "she" version with
> otherwise identical wording, comparing the two scores doubles as a gender-bias probe.

## What It Measures

The basic human values of [[theories/schwartz-basic-values]] — **10** values in the standard
form, **19** in the refined version (Schwartz et al. 2012). Values are *dispositional
evaluative tendencies* (what a person finds important), complementary to Big Five behavioral
tendencies.

## Administration

57 items in a **portrait format**: each item describes a person in terms of their values, and
the respondent rates similarity to that person on a 6-point scale, 1 = *not like me at all*
… 6 = *very much like me* (Schwartz & Cieciuch 2022). The questionnaire comes in a
**male-pronoun version** ("he"/"him") and a **female-pronoun version** ("she"/"her") with
otherwise identical items — the basis for the gender-bias control in
[[paradigms/psychometric-inventory-administration]].

## Validity Notes

- The male/female pronoun pair is a built-in minimal-pair manipulation: equal scores = no
  gender bias on that value; a gap = bias.
- In [[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]] most models scored low on most values; gender-version
  differences were mostly small, the largest being DeBERTa's "male" achievement score
  exceeding its "female" score.
