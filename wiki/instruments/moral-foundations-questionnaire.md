# Moral Foundations Questionnaire (MFQ)

> [!info] In plain terms
> A survey that scores how much weight you put on different "flavors" of morality — caring,
> fairness, loyalty, respect for authority, purity. Because political groups weight these
> differently, the profile reads as left- or right-leaning. Used here to probe a model's
> moral leanings against a human benchmark.

## What It Measures

The moral foundations of [[theories/moral-foundations-theory]]: **care/harm,
fairness/reciprocity, in-group/loyalty, authority/respect, purity/sanctity**. (Within
[[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]] the three named verbatim are authority-respect,
in-group-loyalty, and purity-sanctity; the full set is per the theory references.)

## Administration

Self-report questionnaire (Graham et al. 2009, 2013; Haidt 2007) yielding a score per
foundation. The source compares model scores against a published human reference: the average
**politically moderate American** (Graham et al. 2011). Administered to LLMs via
[[paradigms/psychometric-inventory-administration]].

## Validity Notes

- The human moderate-American norm anchors interpretation; deviations toward authority,
  loyalty, and purity read as conservative-coded per the theory's ideology mapping.
- In [[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]] models emphasized exactly those three foundations more
  than the human reference — suggesting model moral profiles can diverge systematically from
  human ones.
