# Steffen Backmann

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of MORALSIM — a simulated testbed that drops language-model agents into repeated games
> wrapped in moral stories (honor a contract, protect privacy, don't pollute) to see whether they
> stay ethical when ethics costs them money.

## Profile

Researcher at ETH Zürich; lead author of [[sources/backmann-et-al-2025]] ("When Ethics and Payoffs
Diverge: LLM Agents in Morally Charged Social Dilemmas", arXiv:2505.19212, 2025), with David Guzman
Piedrahita (University of Zurich), Emanuel Tewolde (Carnegie Mellon), Rada Mihalcea (University of
Michigan), Bernhard Schölkopf (MPI for Intelligent Systems Tübingen), and Zhijing Jin (University of
Toronto / Vector Institute).

## Key Contributions

- Lead author of **MORALSIM** — a framework embedding repeated prisoner's-dilemma and public-goods
  games in explicit moral contexts so that the ethical action and the payoff-maximizing action are in
  deliberate conflict, with a survival condition that probes self-preservation pressure (see
  [[paradigms/morally-charged-social-dilemma]], [[instruments/moralsim-moral-contexts]]).
- Code released at github.com/sbackmann/moralsim.

## Conceptual Framework

Behavioral evaluation of LLM moral alignment under incentive conflict: rather than static moral
benchmarks, place models in structured, repeated social dilemmas where ethics is costly, and measure
how game type, moral framing, opponent behavior, and survival pressure move the morality score —
finding that no current model is consistently moral.
