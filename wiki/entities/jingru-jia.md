# Jingru Jia

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of work that evaluates how language models reason in strategic games by fitting a
> behavioral model to their choices — recovering a single "how many steps ahead is it thinking?"
> score and using it to expose hidden demographic biases in model behavior.

## Profile

Researcher at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign; lead author of [[sources/jia-et-al-2025]]
("LLM Strategic Reasoning: Agentic Study through Behavioral Game Theory").

## Key Contributions

- Lead author of **"LLM Strategic Reasoning: Agentic Study through Behavioral Game Theory"** (Jia
  et al. 2025) — a behavioral-game-theoretic evaluation framework that fits a Truncated Quantal
  Response Equilibrium model to LLM choices to recover strategic reasoning depth (τ), moving beyond
  binary Nash-Equilibrium checks (see [[paradigms/tqre-strategic-reasoning-depth]]).
- First author (with Yuan, Pan, McNamara, Chen) of the prior **"Decision-making behavior evaluation
  framework for LLMs under uncertain context"** (NeurIPS 2024), extending individual LLM
  decision-evaluation into interactive strategic settings.

## Conceptual Framework

Behavioral-economics evaluation of LLMs: treat a model as an experimental subject, fit
bounded-rationality models (TQRE) to its behavior, and use the recovered parameters both to
characterize reasoning depth and to audit for context- and identity-induced bias.
