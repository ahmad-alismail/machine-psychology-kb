# Yuan Li

> [!info] In plain terms
> Yuan Li is the lead author of a study that builds a broad "psychology exam" for language
> models, testing five mental dimensions at once and checking whether the scores are even
> reliable. The work matters here because it pioneers comparing a model's self-rating against
> how it actually behaves on open-ended scenarios.

## Profile

Lead author of [[sources/li-et-al-2024]], "Evaluating Large Language Models with
Psychometrics" (arXiv:2406.17675, 2024). Affiliated with the University of Cambridge at the
time of the work.

## Key Contributions

- Designed a five-dimension psychometric benchmark (personality, values, emotion, theory of
  mind, motivation/self-efficacy) spanning 13 datasets on nine LLMs.
- Introduced the **closed-form vs. open-ended construct probe**
  ([[paradigms/closed-vs-open-ended-construct-probe]]) — assessing each construct with both a
  self-report item and an open-ended real-world scenario, reading the discrepancy as the result.
- Operationalized a five-form reliability validation suite (internal consistency, parallel
  forms, inter-rater, option-position robustness, adversarial-attack robustness) as a
  precondition for trusting psychological scores read off a model.

## Conceptual Framework

Treats LLMs as respondents in structured psychometric evaluations while stressing that LLMs
"do not 'hold' values but reflect patterned responses based on the data," and that the human-
psychometric assumption of response consistency must be tested, not assumed, for models.

## Affiliations

- Collaborators span University of Notre Dame, Carnegie Mellon, Stanford, and Lehigh.
