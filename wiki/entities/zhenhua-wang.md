# Zhenhua Wang

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of the "Foot In The Door" paper, which explains LLM jailbreaking through
> cognitive psychology and builds an automatic multi-step attack from a classic compliance trick.
> Works at the National University of Defense Technology (China).

## Profile

National University of Defense Technology; lead author of [[sources/wang-et-al-2024]] (with
corresponding author Wei Xie and co-authors at NUDT and the Institute of Information Engineering,
Chinese Academy of Sciences).

## Key Contributions

- Applied **cognitive-psychology methodology** to give interpretability to known LLM jailbreaks,
  re-reading them as cognitive-dissonance-reduction tactics ([[paradigms/jailbreak-tactic-taxonomy]]).
- Designed the **Foot-in-the-Door (FITD)** automatic black-box jailbreak — recursive,
  self-perception-driven escalation of sub-questions ([[paradigms/foot-in-the-door-jailbreak]]),
  averaging 83.9% ASR across 8 advanced LLMs.

## Conceptual Framework

Treats LLM safety refusal as a behavioral consistency phenomenon: a model balances helpfulness
against safety regulations, and attacks succeed by exploiting the same self-consistency and
compliance dynamics psychology documents in humans — positioning jailbreak defense as a
psychology-informed problem.
