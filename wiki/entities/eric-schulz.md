# Eric Schulz

> [!info] In plain terms
> Senior author behind CogBench and a body of work that pioneered using cognitive psychology to
> understand language models. He leads the lab that treats LLMs as experimental subjects in
> psychology-style tasks.

## Profile

Head of the Computational Principles of Intelligence Lab, Max Planck Institute for Biological
Cybernetics (Tübingen) / Helmholtz Munich; senior author of [[sources/coda-forno-et-al-2024]].

## Key Contributions

- Senior author of **CogBench** ([[paradigms/cogbench-cognitive-phenotyping]]).
- Senior author of [[sources/binz-schulz-2022]], **"Using cognitive psychology to understand
  GPT-3"** — among the first to run heuristics-and-biases probes on an LLM (see
  [[paradigms/heuristics-and-biases-battery]]) and the source of the horizon/two-step prompting
  protocols CogBench reuses, as well as the contamination + surface-form critique
  ([[paradigms/adversarial-vignette-perturbation]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Computational cognitive modeling applied to artificial agents: characterize *how* a model learns and
decides by fitting reinforcement-learning and Bayesian models to its behavior.

## Affiliations

Computational Principles of Intelligence Lab — Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics;
Helmholtz Munich.
