# Xiachong Feng

> [!info] In plain terms
> Corresponding author of TMGBench — a benchmark that tests language models on every kind of simple
> two-player game, rewrites each as a story to stop memorization, and stacks games into harder
> structures to expose where strategic reasoning breaks down.

## Profile

Researcher at The University of Hong Kong; corresponding author of [[sources/guo-et-al-2026]]
("Game-theoretic evaluation of strategic reasoning in large language models: From complete coverage
to compositional complexity", *Neurocomputing* 2026), with Yu Guo and Haochuan Wang (Harbin
Institute of Technology).

## Key Contributions

- Corresponding/senior author of **TMGBench** — the first benchmark to cover all 144 canonical 2×2
  game types of the [[theories/robinson-goforth-topology]], pairing exhaustive coverage with synthetic
  narrative reskins (contamination control) and compositional sequential/parallel/nested structures
  (see [[paradigms/compositional-game-reasoning]], [[instruments/tmgbench]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Game-theoretic behavioral evaluation of LLMs: probe strategic reasoning across the *complete* space
of game structures rather than a few famous games, control contamination with human-validated
synthetic scenarios, and scale difficulty via programmatic composition — reading out exact
Nash-equilibrium accuracy plus symmetry-based bias metrics.
