# Yu Lei

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of FairMindSim, a study that drops both real people and language-model "agents" into the
> same unfair-money game to see who pays a cost to punish unfairness — and finds GPT-4o enforces
> fairness more than humans do while holding a steadier belief in it.

## Profile

Lead author of [[sources/lei-et-al-2024]] ("FairMindSim: Alignment of Behavior, Emotion, and Belief
in Humans and LLM Agents Amid Ethical Dilemmas"); the work was performed while Yu Lei was a research
assistant at Tsinghua University. Co-authors include Hao Liu, Chengxing Xie, Songjia Liu, Zhiyu Yin,
Canyu Chen, Guohao Li, Philip Torr, and corresponding author Zhen Wu (Tsinghua), spanning Tsinghua,
Oxford, KAUST, Fudan, IIT, Stevens, and the CAMEL-AI org.

## Key Contributions

- Built **FairMindSim** — a third-party-punishment / unfair-allocation simulation that probes whether
  an LLM agent will pay a cost to enforce fairness, run on matched human and agent personas
  ([[paradigms/altruistic-punishment-game]]).
- Proposed the **Belief-Reward Alignment Behavior Evolution Model (BREM)**, built on recursive reward
  modeling, to fit a latent "belief in fairness/justice" and its weighting against reward from
  sequential choices — separating value-driven from reward-driven behavior in humans and LLMs.

## Conceptual Framework

Treats LLM agents as experimental subjects in classic behavioral-economics paradigms and compares
them to a human baseline on three parallel channels — behavior, emotion, and belief — to ask whether
a model's social/moral values align with humans' and whether they evolve over interaction.
