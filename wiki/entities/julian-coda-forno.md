# Julian Coda-Forno

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of CogBench — the project that runs language models through seven classic
> psychology-lab experiments to profile *how* they think, not just how well they score.

## Profile

Computational Principles of Intelligence Lab, Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics
(Tübingen) and Institute for Human-Centered AI, Helmholtz Munich; lead and corresponding author of
[[sources/coda-forno-et-al-2024]] (julian.coda-forno@helmholtz-munich.de). Co-authors: Marcel Binz,
Jane X. Wang (Google DeepMind), Eric Schulz ([[entities/eric-schulz]]).

## Key Contributions

- Built **CogBench** ([[paradigms/cogbench-cognitive-phenotyping]], [[instruments/cogbench]]): the
  first holistic, standardized battery of cognitive-psychology experiments for behaviorally
  phenotyping LLMs, with procedurally-generated items to resist contamination.
- Showed (with co-authors) that **RLHF makes models more human-like and raises meta-cognition**,
  **model size drives model-basedness**, and — counter to expectation — **open-source models take
  less risk than proprietary ones**.
- Prior single-task machine-psychology work: "Inducing anxiety in large language models increases
  exploration and bias" (Coda-Forno et al. 2023) and "Meta-in-context learning in large language
  models" (Coda-Forno et al. 2024).
- Co-author of [[sources/akata-et-al-2023]], **"Playing repeated games with Large Language
  Models"** — behavioral game theory for machines via finitely repeated 2×2 games
  ([[paradigms/repeated-games]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Cognitive-psychology-grounded, behavioral (input→output) evaluation of LLMs, using computational
cognitive modeling to derive a quantitative behavioral phenotype rather than relying on performance
metrics alone.
