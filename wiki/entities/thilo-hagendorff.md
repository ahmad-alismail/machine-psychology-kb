# Thilo Hagendorff

> [!info] In plain terms
> Thilo Hagendorff is the researcher most associated with coining and popularizing the term "machine psychology" — the idea of studying AI language models with the experimental toolkit of psychology. His framing is constructive and optimistic: psychology's methods can teach us a lot about how these models behave.

## Profile
Researcher at the University of Stuttgart working at the intersection of AI ethics, AI safety, and the behavioral study of large language models. He is the shared first author (with Ishita Dasgupta) of the foundational "Machine Psychology" review and author of the earlier arXiv piece that introduced "machine psychology" as an umbrella term (Hagendorff, 2023).

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/hagendorff-et-al-2024]] — the foundational review proposing and structuring the machine-psychology research program (four research areas; hypothesis-driven behavioral method; methodological cautions on contamination, the performance–competence distinction, self-report sensitivity, and anthropomorphism).
- Empirical work on human-like intuition and reasoning biases in GPT-family models (cited within the review), and on LLMs' emerging capacity to induce false beliefs in other agents.

## Conceptual Framework
Hagendorff classifies the field as a **behavioral, experiment-driven** discipline: LLMs are treated as participants in psychology experiments, and explanation proceeds via input–output analysis rather than internal (mechanistic) inspection. He frames psychological vocabulary as offering "thick descriptions" that add real explanatory power to black-box models even absent direct neural correlates. His stance is constructive — he sees machine psychology as a valuable addition to traditional NLP benchmarking and to AI safety/alignment — while urging caution about anthropomorphism and training-data contamination. This optimistic framing contrasts with the stricter, validity-first stance of [[entities/niklas-kiehne]] (see [[debates/test-validity-sufficiency]]) and the measurement/trait orientation of [[entities/max-pellert]] (see [[debates/machine-psychology-terminology]]).
