# Max Pellert

> [!info] In plain terms
> Computational social scientist and lead author of the paper that proposed "AI
> psychometrics" — using human personality and values questionnaires as diagnostic tools on
> language models.

## Profile

Business School, University of Mannheim; corresponding and lead author of
[[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]]. Co-authors: Clemens M. Lechner, Claudia Wagner, Beatrice
Rammstedt (GESIS / RWTH Aachen), and Markus Strohmaier (Univ. Mannheim / GESIS / Complexity
Science Hub Vienna).

## Key Contributions

- Proposed and demonstrated **"AI psychometrics"** — repurposing standard human psychometric
  inventories as diagnostic tools for LLM traits, using zero-shot NLI elicitation
  ([[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]]; see [[theories/ai-psychometrics]] and
  [[paradigms/psychometric-inventory-administration]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Behavioral, input→output profiling of LLMs through validated human inventories; emphasis on
open, reproducible setups (open models + replication materials) and explicit caution against
anthropomorphizing models as more than "prediction machines."
