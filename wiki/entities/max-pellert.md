# Max Pellert

> [!info] In plain terms
> Max Pellert is a computational social scientist who proposes "AI psychometrics": using established human questionnaires to profile the personalities, values, and beliefs that language models pick up from their training text. His emphasis is on measurement and trait profiling rather than psychology-style experiments.

## Profile
Researcher affiliated with the Business School at the University of Mannheim, GESIS–Leibniz Institute for the Social Sciences, and the Complexity Science Hub Vienna. Corresponding and lead author of the "AI Psychometrics" article in *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, working at the intersection of psychology, computational social science, and natural language processing.

## Key Contributions
- [[sources/pellert-et-al-2024]] — coins/advances "AI psychometrics" as the program of assessing noncognitive traits (personality, values, morality, gender/sex beliefs) of LLMs via repurposed psychometric inventories, demonstrated with zero-shot NLI classification on seven encoder models.
- Empirical findings that LLM personality profiles are notably homogeneous and that models lean toward conservative-associated moral foundations.

## Conceptual Framework
Pellert classifies the work as **psychometric measurement / trait profiling**, not psychology experimentation. LLMs are framed as having metaphorically "acquired" traits sedimented in their training corpora, which can be elicited as scale scores having "no ground truth" (paralleling the human distinction between abilities and traits). Crucially, he does **not** use the term "machine psychology"; he positions AI psychometrics within Rahwan et al.'s **"machine behavior"** research program. This measurement-and-trait emphasis distinguishes him from the experiment-and-process framing of [[entities/thilo-hagendorff]]. See [[debates/machine-psychology-terminology]].
