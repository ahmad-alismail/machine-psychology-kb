# Fulin Guo

> [!info] In plain terms
> Sole author of an early study that put GPT-4 into two classic economics games — the ultimatum game
> and the prisoner's dilemma — to see whether it plays like a person, how an assigned "fair" or
> "selfish" personality changes its play, and what its written-out reasoning reveals about its (often
> flawed) strategic logic.

## Profile

Researcher at the Faculty of Economics, University of Cambridge; sole author of [[sources/guo-2023]]
("GPT in Game Theory Experiments", arXiv:2305.05516, econ.GN, 2023). Not to be confused with the Yu
Guo of [[sources/guo-et-al-2026]] (TMGBench).

## Key Contributions

- **Persona-prompted strategic-game experiments on GPT-4** — establishes the template of running
  canonical repeated economic games on an LLM while inducing a named trait ("fairness concern" vs.
  "selfishness") via a single-word prompt change, and reading the behavior against human-subject
  baselines (see [[paradigms/persona-prompted-strategic-games]], [[instruments/guo-game-prompts]]).
- **Elicited-reasoning content analysis** — pioneers having GPT emit a reasoning string with each
  decision and coding those statements (LLM-as-classifier + manual validation) into theory-derived
  categories, surfacing the round-3 rejection peak's logic and ~80% judgment-error rate in final-round
  cooperation.

## Conceptual Framework

GPT as a subject in behavioral/experimental economics: a controllable, low-cost complement to human
game-theory experiments whose verbalized reasoning offers a window into decision rationale, and whose
prompt-steerable traits point toward large-scale social simulation.
