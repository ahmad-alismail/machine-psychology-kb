# Elif Akata

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of the study that had several language models play classic two-player games
> over and over — against each other, scripted opponents, and real people — to map out
> their cooperation and coordination behavior.

## Profile

Lead and corresponding author of [[sources/akata-et-al-2023]] (elif.akata@uni-tuebingen.de).
Affiliated with the Institute for Human-Centered AI (Helmholtz Munich), the Max Planck
Institute for Biological Cybernetics (Tübingen), and the University of Tübingen; supported
by the International Max Planck Research School for Intelligent Systems (IMPRS-IS). She
conceived and conducted the experiments and led the analysis and writing. Co-authors: Lion
Schulz, Julian Coda-Forno ([[entities/julian-coda-forno]]), Seong Joon Oh, Matthias Bethge,
Eric Schulz ([[entities/eric-schulz]]).

## Key Contributions

- Lead author of **"Playing repeated games with Large Language Models"** — a behavioral
  game theory *for machines*: five LLMs play finitely repeated 2×2 games, revealing that
  they excel at self-interested games (Prisoner's Dilemma family) but fail at coordination
  (Battle of the Sexes), and that **social chain-of-thought (SCoT)** prompting repairs the
  coordination failure (see [[paradigms/repeated-games]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Behavioral game theory applied to LLMs: use tightly controlled, theoretically well-understood
iterated games to read social-behavioral signatures (cooperation, retaliation, coordination)
from move sequences, and intervene on them via prompting.
