# Desmond C. Ong

> [!info] In plain terms
> A psychologist who wrote the short review that sorts LLM cognitive-science research into three
> families — and lays out the methodological traps each one falls into.

## Profile

Department of Psychology, The University of Texas at Austin. Sole author of [[sources/ong-2024]].

## Key Contributions

- Proposed a **typology of three research paradigms** for studying LLMs in cognitive science —
  [[paradigms/gpt-ology]], [[paradigms/llm-as-computational-model]], [[paradigms/silicon-sampling]]
  — unified by one base experiment ("an LLM responds to some input") and distinguished by the
  *inference* each draws ([[sources/ong-2024]]).
- Articulated the **Outstanding Issues** governing the validity of these evaluations: closed-source
  dependence, training-data leakage, prompt brittleness, and embracing stochasticity via sampling
  rather than temperature=0.

## Conceptual Framework

Meta-scientific: focuses on "scientific reliability and validity, logical deductions, and epistemic
support" rather than adjudicating specific psychological claims. Emphasizes the asymmetric logic of
existence proofs (presence licenses sufficient-conditions claims; absence does not license
necessary-conditions claims) and treating LLM responses as samples from a distribution.
