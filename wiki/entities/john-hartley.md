# John Hartley

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of the study that measured GPT-4o's risk appetite with a behavioural-economics
> gamble test and showed its risk-taking can be steered by prompting it with Big Five
> personality traits.

## Profile

NatWest AI Research; corresponding and lead author of [[sources/hartley-et-al-2024]]
(john.hartley@natwest.com). Co-authors: Conor Hamill, Devesh Batra, Dale Seddon (NatWest AI
Research), Ramin Okhrati and Raad Khraishi (University College London; Khraishi also NatWest).

## Key Contributions

- Introduced a **direct-reporting certainty-equivalent method** for estimating an LLM's
  cumulative-prospect-theory risk parameters, improving stability over prior bracketing methods
  ([[paradigms/certainty-equivalent-cpt-estimation]]).
- Demonstrated that **Big-Five personality interventions causally shift an LLM's risk-propensity**,
  with Openness the most influential trait — mirroring human patterns in GPT-4o
  ([[paradigms/personality-intervention-risk-propensity]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Behavioural, input→output evaluation of LLM decision-making under risk via behavioural economics
(CPT) and personality psychology (Big Five), aimed at building risk-propensity-calibrated agents
for financial simulations.
