# Julia Hirschberg

> [!info] In plain terms
> A senior computer scientist at Columbia University who leads its speech-and-language NLP group.
> She is the senior author of a 2025 survey mapping how psychological theories are used across
> the whole life of a language model — including how they are used to *evaluate* model behavior.

## Profile

Percy K. and Vida L. W. Hudson Professor of Computer Science, Columbia University; senior author
of [[sources/liu-et-al-2025]] ("The Mind in the Machine"). The paper is a cross-departmental
Columbia / Barnard College collaboration: the five equal-contribution lead authors (Zizhou Liu,
Ziwei Gong, Lin Ai, Zheng Hui, Run Chen) are from Columbia Computer Science, with psychologists
Colin Wayne Leach and Michelle R. Greene from Barnard.

## Key Contributions

- Senior author of a lifecycle-spanning survey of psychology-in-LLMs, whose evaluation-stage
  catalog and validity-debate section are the parts relevant to this wiki
  ([[sources/liu-et-al-2025]]; see [[theories/operant-conditioning]],
  [[theories/theory-of-mind]], [[theories/big-five-personality]]).

## Conceptual Framework

Bridges NLP and psychology — advocating that psychological theory should inform LLM data,
training, and evaluation, while warning against terminology mismatch and over-anthropomorphizing
model "attention" and "memory."

## Affiliations

Columbia University, Department of Computer Science (Speech & NLP).
