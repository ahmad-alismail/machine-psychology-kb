# Marcel Binz

> [!info] In plain terms
> Lead author of one of the first studies to probe a language model with cognitive-psychology
> experiments, and a co-author of CogBench. He helped establish the practice of treating an LLM as
> an experimental subject and fitting cognitive models to its behavior.

## Profile

Researcher at the Computational Principles of Intelligence Lab, Max Planck Institute for Biological
Cybernetics (Tübingen) / Helmholtz Munich; lead author of [[sources/binz-schulz-2022]] and co-author
of [[sources/coda-forno-et-al-2024]] (CogBench).

## Key Contributions

- Lead author of **"Using cognitive psychology to understand GPT-3"** (Binz & Schulz 2022) — among
  the first to run heuristics-and-biases, exploration, two-step, and causal-reasoning probes on an
  LLM, and to articulate the **contamination + surface-form robustness** critique of vignette-based
  evaluation (see [[paradigms/adversarial-vignette-perturbation]]).
- Co-author of **CogBench** ([[paradigms/cogbench-cognitive-phenotyping]]), which reuses the
  horizon- and two-step-task prompting protocols established in the GPT-3 paper.

## Conceptual Framework

Computational cognitive modeling of artificial agents: characterize *how* a model decides by fitting
reinforcement-learning and decision-theoretic models (prospect theory, model-based/model-free RL,
directed/random exploration) to its trial-by-trial behavior.
